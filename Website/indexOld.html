Can I Eat That?

aboutMe

Can I Eat That? is a Twitter bot and an Amazon Echo application that allows you to quickly and easily figure out if you are allowed, in fact, to consume that item.

This app is powered by Lambda, Python, Javascript, and JSON.



A lesser known fact about America is that an astounding 7 in 10 Americans struggle with dietary restrictions every day. Oftentimes at a party or family outing, they are unsure of what lies in the food they might eat. To help these individuals, our team has created a dietary assistant. This app helps people to determine what meals meet their dietary needs. Not only are those with allergies assisted by our creation, but those with religious limitations are catered to as well. Individuals who can only consume Halal or Kosher items are able to find locations near them that offer food that is up to their religious standards.

projectLinks

Check out our Twitter bot page here.
To use the bot, log into Twitter and tweet @dietaryasst and list your dietary restrictions (e.g. "@dietaryasst gluten-free"). This will enable the assistant to cater to you in the future. Afterwards, to use the assistant, tweet "@dietaryasst [food] [location]" to receive notifications based on your diet.

 Check out our Amazon Echo application page here.
To use the Amazon Echo application, simply say Alexa's name, list your dietary restrictions, and ask your question.
e.g. “Alexa, I am lactose intolerant. Can I order a cheese pizza at Domino’s?”
